About Griz (the main character):

Griz is the main character of the book A Boy and His Dog at the End of the World. At the start of the book, Griz explained the current world situation of only having a couple thousand people spread out throught the entire world. Throught the book, Griz chased revenge against someone who had lied to him before by stealing his dog. However, by the end, Griz's views on the world and the people around him vastly changed to how they were at the start of the book. Griz battled with the idea of trusting others due to the fear of others lying to them.

Author Message:

The author's, Charlie Fletcher's, main idea and theme of the book is to display how one is affected by revenge. Throught the entire book, Charlie Fletcher constantly showed the tarnishment of one's morality as they pursue their personal revenge on others. For example, near the end of the novel, Griz was captured by the Cons while trying to catch Brand. After lying to the Cons, Griz had said "That made the lie easier to tell."(Fletcher 335) At the start of the novel, Griz had the idea that lying was horrible instilled into him by everyone around him. However, when Griz was caught by the Cons because of his quest for revenge, he lied to them, showing his change from the start of the novel. On Griz's path to his revenge, he had started to change, esspecially on what he would do in certain situations. Griz's morals were completly changed for the worse when he lied to others due to him pursuing his revenge. Earlier in the novel, Griz had met John Dark in the middle of the forest. John Dark was on her own path for revenge where she was trying to find the person who had killed her daughters.When John Dark had found Griz and saved him from a bull, Griz stated, "I did understand what the gun meant when it lowered and pointed at me"(Fletcher 182). John Dark had a lot of principles, for example, her being kind to others and being sympathetic towards others. When John Dark met Griz, they were in a very vulnerable position where they were trapped and had recently lost the dog they had spent the last few days. Since she “lowered” the gun and "pointed" it at Griz, she showed her dedication to controlling Griz by threatening him even though her own family was also harmed by someone else she was currently trying to find. John Dark took advantage of Griz when he was trapped and had just lost his dog by controlling him with the gun that she had. By controlling Griz with the gun that she had and taking advantage of Griz's helpless position, John Dark spoiled her morality of being kind and respectful to others by controlling Griz due to her personal hunt for revenge.
